What I'm focused on right now. What is this?

Working at a law firm

In a shocking turn of events, I've been working at a law firm in San Francisco since September 2024.

My official title is "Legal Assistant"; in practice I maintain the legal calendar, do paralegal work, and write critical internal software the attorneys rely on!

The backstory: Bryan Johnson officially took over Blueprint, so the operation moved to LA. I had just met my girlfriend (now wife!) and wanted to stay in SF. Then this job fell into my lap.

The legal industry is a JUICY target for automation

The whole business is one giant bundle of back-office processes: Email and (some) physical mail comes in, filings and phone calls go out, attorneys hop on Zoom hearings, money flows through.

How long until the first fully automated law firm?

A human still has to remain in the loop to be ultimately responsible, but the tech to do this exists today. I don't code on this full-time, but I'll keep pushing in that direction until the attorneys tell me to stop automating.

I married my girlfriend in February. We met at a party on New Year's Day 2024, her birthday, and everything kind of fell into place from there.

Also we adopted two Siamese kittens: Tleilax and Caladan.

Life is good.
